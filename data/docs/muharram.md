# Muharram

**Muharram**[a] ([Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language): ٱلْمُحَرَّم, romanized: al-Muḥarram) is the first month of the [Islamic calendar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_calendar). It is one of the four sacred months of the year when warfare is banned. It precedes the month of [Safar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Safar). The tenth of Muharram is known as [Ashura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashura), an important day of commemoration in [Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam). For [Sunni Muslims](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunni_Islam), the day commemorates the [parting of the Red Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crossing_the_Red_Sea) by [Moses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moses_in_Islam) and the salvation of the [Israelites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israelites), observed through voluntary [fasting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fasting) and other permissible expressions of gratitude. By contrast, Ashura is a day of mourning for [Shia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shia_Islam) Muslims, who annually commemorate the death of [Husayn ibn Ali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Husayn_ibn_Ali), grandson of the [Islamic prophet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prophets_and_messengers_in_Islam) [Muhammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad) and the third [Shia imam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imamate_in_Shia_doctrine). Husayn was killed, alongside most of his relatives and his small retinue, in the [Battle of Karbala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Karbala) in 680 [CE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_Era) against the army of the [Umayyad caliph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate) [Yazid ibn Mu'awiya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yazid_I) (r. 680–683). The Shia rituals span the first ten days of Muharram, culminating on Ashura with mourning processions in Shia cities. Also in Muharram, the [Aqsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Aqsa_Mosque) mosque in [Jerusalem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jerusalem) was initially set as the direction of prayer for early Muslims.

## Islamic events

- **1 Muharram** — Death of Caliph [Umar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umar) (r. 634–644) by injuries from the attack of Persian slave [Abu Lu'lu'a Firuz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abu_Lu'lu'a_Firuz).[28] Sunnis carry rallies on 1 Muharram to commemorate Umar.[29]
- **2–10 Muharram** — Most mourning rituals for Karbala take place during the first ten days of Muharram, culminating on the tenth with processions in major Shia cities.[30][31]
- **5 Muharram** — [Baba Farid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baba_Farid), a [Punjabi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punjab) [Sufi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sufism) saint, died on this day in 1266. His death is celebrated (*urs*) for six days during Muharram, in [Pakpattan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakpattan), [Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan).[32]
- **8 Muharram** — In what became known as the 1782 [Muharram Rebellion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muharram_Rebellion), on this day [Bengali Muslims](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengali_Muslims) in [Sylhet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sylhet) staged one of the earliest anti-British uprisings in the [Indian subcontinent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_subcontinent).[33][34]
- **16 Muharram** — On this day, the Islamic prophet Muhammad set the [Aqsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Aqsa_Mosque) mosque in [Jerusalem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jerusalem) as the qibla, towards which early Muslims prayed.[1] This was superseded later by the ancient [Ka'ba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaaba) sanctuary in [Mecca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mecca) in connection with verse 2:144 of the [Quran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quran), the central religious text in Islam.[35]
- **17 Muharram** — Arrival of the "people of the elephant" in Mecca, a reference to al-Fil (lit. 'the elephant'), a surah (chapter) in the Quran.[1]
