# Dhu'l-Hijja

**Dhu'l-Hijja**[a] ([Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language): ‏ذُو ٱلْحِجَّة‎, romanized: Ḏū l-Ḥijja IPA: [ðu‿l.ħid͡ʒːa]) is the twelfth and final month in the [Islamic calendar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_calendar).[1] Being one of the four [sacred months](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacred_months) during which war is forbidden, it is the month in which the *Ḥajj* ([Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language): حج, lit. 'pilgrimage') takes place as well as [Eid al-Adha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eid_al-Adha) ([Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language): عيد الأضحى, lit. 'the Festival of the Sacrifice').

The [Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language) name of the month, *Dhu'l-Hijja*, means "Possessor of the Pilgrimage" or "The Month of the Pilgrimage".[1] During this month, Muslim pilgrims from all around the world congregate at [Mecca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mecca) to visit the [Kaaba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaaba). The [Hajj rites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hajj#Rites) begin on the eighth day and continue for four or five days. The [Day of Arafah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Day_of_Arafah) takes place on the ninth of the month. [Eid al-Adha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eid_al-Adha), the "Festival of the Sacrifice", begins on the tenth day and ends on the thirteenth day.[1]

## Islamic events

- **18 Dhu'l-Hijja** — [assassination of Uthman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assassination_of_Uthman), the prominent [companion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahaba) and son-in-law of [Muhammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prophet_Muhammad) and [Khadija](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khadija_bint_Khuwaylid). Husband of [Ruqayya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruqayya_bint_Muhammad) and [Umm Kulthum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umm_Kulthum_bint_Muhammad).
- **01 Dhu'l-Hijja** — *nikah* ([marriage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_marital_jurisprudence)) of [Ali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ali) and [Fatima](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatima) – AH 2 (24 February AD 624).
- **07 Dhu'l-Hijja** — martyrdom of [Twelver](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twelver) and [Ismāʿīlī](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isma'ilism) [Shīʿite Imām](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imamah_(Shi'a_doctrine)), [Muhammad al-Bāqir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_al-Baqir) – AH 114.
- **08 Dhu'l-Hijja** — [Husayn ibn ʿAlī](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Husayn_ibn_Ali) began his journey to [Karbalāʾ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Karbala) from Mecca.
- **09 Dhu'l-Hijja** — martyrdom of [Muslim ibn ʿAqīl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim_ibn_Aqeel) and Hani ibn Urwah in [Kufah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kufa). It is also a day of [supererogatory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supererogation) [fasting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fasting) – AH 60.
- **12 Dhu'l-Hijja or 18 Dhu'l-Hijja** — assassination of [Uthman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uthman).
- **15 Dhu'l-Hijja** — birth of [Twelver Imām](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twelver), [ʿAlī al-Naqī](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ali_al-Hadi) – AH 214 [Disputed date].
- **18 Dhu'l-Hijja** — [Shīʿite Muslims](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shia_Islam) celebrate [the event of Ghadir Khumm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_event_of_Ghadir_Khumm) – AH 10.
- **19 Dhu'l-Hijja** — Fatimah went to Ali's house after their marriage.
- **23 Dhu'l-Hijja** — martyrdom of [Meesam Tammar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meesam_Tammar), friend of Ali – AH 60.
- **23 Dhu'l-Hijja** — martyrdom of two sons of Muslim ibn ʿAqīl in Kufa – AH 60.
- **24 Dhu'l-Hijja** — [event of the mubahala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Event_of_the_mubahala) took place ('[Eid al-Mubahilah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eid_al-Mubahila)).
- **24 Dhu'l-Hijja** — some historians mention that the [Hadith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadith), [Ahl al-Kisa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahl_al-Kisa)', event was also on the same day prior to Muhammad setting out for Mubahila.
- **24 Dhu'l-Hijja** — supplication day and giving of alms with the ring by Ali. In reply verse, "Verily your *Walee* is Allah; and His Messenger and those who establish *salah*, and pay *zakat* while they be in *Rukooʿ*. (Maa-Idah: 55)" was revealed.
- **25 Dhu'l-Hijja** — [Sura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sura) [Al-Insan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Insan) or Hal Ata, or Dahar, which records the giving of alms to orphans, the destitute and travellers by Fatimah [Hasan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hasan_ibn_Ali) and Husain was revealed.
- **25 Dhu'l-Hijja** — Ali becomes the Caliph of Islam – AH 35.[citation needed]
