# Rabi II

**Rabi II** ([Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language): ‏رَبِيع الثَّانِي‎, romanized: Rabīʿ ath-Thānī,[a] lit. 'Rabi the Second', more commonly called ‏رَبِيع الْآخِر‎ Rabīʿ al-ʾĀkhir,[b] lit. 'Rabi the Last')[c] is the fourth month of the [Islamic calendar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_calendar). The name *Rabīʿ ath-Thānī* means "the second spring" in [Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic), referring to its position in the [pre-Islamic Arabian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pre-Islamic_Arabia) calendar.

## Islamic events

- **8 or 10 Rabi II** — the birth of the [Eleventh Imam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imamate_(Twelver_doctrine)) [Hasan al-Askari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hasan_al-Askari)
- **10 or 12 Rabi II** — death of [Fatimah bint Musa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatimah_bint_Musa)
- **11 Rabi II** — death of [Abdul-Qadir Gilani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdul-Qadir_Gilani), the [Sufi sheikh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sufism) who is believed to be the "saint of saints"
- **27 Rabi II** — death of [Ahmad Sirhindi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmad_Sirhindi)
- **28 or 29 Rabi II** — death of [ibn Arabi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibn_Arabi), the great philosopher from [Andalus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Andalus) who died and rests in [Damascus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damascus), Syria.[3]
