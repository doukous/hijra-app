# Rajab

**Rajab** ([Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language): رَجَب) is the seventh month of the [Islamic calendar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_calendar). The lexical definition of the [classical Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_Arabic) verb *rajaba* is "to respect",[1] which could also mean "be awe or be in fear", of which Rajab is a derivative.

This month is regarded as one of the four sacred months (including [Muharram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muharram), [Dhu'l-Qa'da](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhu'l-Qa'da) and [Dhu'l-Hijja](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhu'l-Hijja)) in Islam in which battles are prohibited. The [pre-Islamic Arabs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pre-Islamic_Arabia) also considered warfare to be blasphemous during these four months.[1]

Muslims believe Rajab is the month in which [‘Alī ibn Abī Tālib](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/‘Alī_ibn_Abī_Tālib), the first Shia Imam and the fourth [Rashidun caliph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashidun_caliph), was born.

## Islamic events

- The [Battle of Tabouk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Tabouk) took place in Rajab, 9 AH (October 630).
- The [Second pledge at al-Aqabah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_pledge_at_al-Aqabah) took place in Rajab.
- **6 Rajab** — Many [Sufi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sufi) followers of the [Chishti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chishti) tariqa (path) celebrate the anniversary of Khawaja [Moinuddin Chishti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moinuddin_Chishti).
- **7 Rajab** — [Twelvers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twelvers) observe the Festival of Imam Musa al-Kazim in dedication of [Musā' al-Kādhim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musa_al-Kadhim). This is so as to avoid missing celebrating the birth of the seventh imam, which took place in Safar.
- **22 Rajab** — In India and Pakistan, Koonday (tablecloth dinner) is organized. It is an occasion for Muslims to discuss [Allah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allah) and the [Ahlul Bayt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahlul_Bayt) and to strengthen ties among the Sunni Sufi community with love and compassion.
- **27 Rajab** — event of *Isra and Mi'raj*.
- **27 Rajab 583 AH** — [Conquest of Jerusalem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Jerusalem_(1187))[8] by the [Ayyubids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ayyubids)
