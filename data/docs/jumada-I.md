# Jumada I

**Jumada I** ([Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language): ‏جُمَادَى ٱلْأُولَى‎, romanized: Jumādā l-ʾŪlā,[a] lit. 'Jumada the First') is the fifth month of the [Islamic calendar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_calendar). Jumada I spans 29 or 30 days. The origin of the month's name is theorized by some as coming from the word *jamād* ([Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language): جماد), meaning "arid, dry, or cold",[1] denoting the dry and parched land and hence the dry months of the [pre-Islamic Arabian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pre-Islamic_Arabia) calendar. *Jumādā* ([Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language): جُمَادَى) may also be related to a verb meaning "to freeze", and another account relates that water would freeze during this time of year. The secondary name *Jumādā l-ʾŪlā* may possibly mean "to take charge with, commend, entrust, commit or care during the arid or cold month".[1] However, this explanation is rejected by some as *Jumādā al-ʾŪlā* is a lunar month that does not coincide with seasons as solar months do.

In Turkish, the name of the month is *cemaziyelevvel* (from [Ottoman Turkish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Turkish_language): جمادی الاول, romanized: cemaziyü'l-evvel);[2][3] it was abbreviated as جا *ca* in [Ottoman Turkish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Turkish).[2][4]

## Islamic events

- **On 1 Jumada I 8 AH (September 629)** — the [Battle of Mu'ta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Mu'tah) took place in the [village of the same name](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mu'tah) (in present-day [Jordan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jordan)), in which the Muslim army (dispatched by [Muhammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad)) fought against the army of the [Byzantine Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_Empire_under_the_Heraclian_dynasty) and their [Ghassanid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghassanids) vassals reportedly in retribution for the murder of a Muslim emissary by a Ghassanid chief, [Shurahbil ibn Amr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shurahbil_ibn_Amr). The battle was won by the Byzantines, and amongst the losses the Muslims incurred were three of the most prominent [Companions of Muhammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Companions_of_Muhammad_(Sahabah)): [Zayd ibn Haritha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zayd_ibn_Haritha_al-Kalbi) (his adopted son, known as the fourth person to become a Muslim); [Ja‘far ibn Abi Talib](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ja'far_ibn_Abi_Talib) (his cousin), and [‘Abd Allah ibn Rawaha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abd_Allah_ibn_Rawahah) (present at the [second pledge at al-‘Aqaba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_pledge_at_al-Aqabah)), whom Muhammad had specifically appointed to be generals of the Muslim army in the battle, so that if one of them should be killed, the other would take his place.[6]
- **On 5 Jumada I** — [Zaynab bint Ali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zaynab_bint_Ali) was born.
- **On 8 Jumada I** — Amir al-Kulal died. [Amir Kulal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amir_Kulal).
- **On 10 Jumada I 11 AH** — [Fatima](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatima), daughter of [Muhammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad), died in Medina at the young age of 23 years according to Sunni Muslim sources.
- **On 13 Jumada I 11 AH** — Fatima was buried by her husband [Ali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ali).
- **On 15 Jumada I** — [Ali ibn Husayn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ali_ibn_Husayn) was born.
- **On 20 Jumada I 857** — [Mehmed II conquered Constantinople](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_Constantinople).
