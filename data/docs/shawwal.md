# Shawwal

**Shawwal** ([Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language): شَوَّال, romanized: Shawwāl) is the tenth month of the [Islamic calendar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_calendar). It comes after [Ramadan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ramadan) and before [Dhu'l-Qa'da](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhu'l-Qa'da).

*Shawwāl* stems from the Arabic verb *shāla* (شَالَ), which means to 'lift or carry',[1] generally to take or move things from one place to another.

## Islamic events

- **Every 1st day of Shawwāl** — [Eid al-Fitr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eid_al-Fitr) is celebrated throughout the [Muslim World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ummah) for the first three days of this month (but celebrations last until the 29th/30th day as per the festive season's duration).
- **7 Shawwāl 3 AH** — early Muslims took part in the [Battle of Uhud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Uhud).
- **10 Shawwal** — birth of [Ahmed Raza Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmed_Raza_Khan).
- **13 Shawwāl** — primary traditionist of the Sunni Muslims, [Muhammad al-Bukhari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_al-Bukhari), was born in 194 AH.
- **18 Shawwal** — [urs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urs) of Sufi Mystic And Poet [Amir Khusro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amir_Khusro).
- **22 Shawwāl 1284 AH** — death of [Haji Dost Muhammad Qandhari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haji_Dost_Muhammad_Qandhari), an Afghan Sufi master of [Naqshbandi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naqshbandi) tradition.
- **25 Shawwāl 148 AH** — martyrdom of Imām [Ja‘far as-Sādiq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ja'far_al-Sadiq).
