# Ramadan

**Ramadan**[a][b] is the ninth month of the [Islamic calendar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_calendar). It is observed by [Muslims](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim) worldwide as a month of [fasting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fasting) (*sawm*), communal [prayer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prayer) (*salah*), reflection, study of the Quran, charity, and strengthening community ties.[5] It is also the month in which the [Quran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quran) is believed to have been revealed to the [Islamic prophet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prophets_of_Islam) [Muhammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad), known as [Laylat al-Qadr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Night_of_Power). The annual observance of Ramadan is regarded as one of the [five pillars of Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Five_pillars_of_Islam)[6] and lasts 29 to 30 days, from one sighting of the [crescent moon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hilal_(crescent_moon)) to the next.[7][8]

Fasting from dawn to sunset is obligatory (*fard*) for all Muslims who have reached puberty,[9] and who are not [acutely](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acute_illness) or [chronically ill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chronic_illness), [traveling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travel), [elderly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_age), [breastfeeding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breastfeeding), [pregnant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pregnancy), or [menstruating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Menstruation_in_Islam).[10] The predawn meal is *suhur*, and the nightly feast that breaks the fast is *iftar*.[11][12] Although rulings (*fatawa*) have been issued that Muslims who live in regions with a [midnight sun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midnight_sun) or [polar night](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polar_night) should follow the timetable of [Mecca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mecca),[13] it is common practice to follow the timetable of the closest country in which night can be distinguished from day.[14][15][16]

The spiritual rewards (*thawab*) of fasting are believed to be multiplied during Ramadan.[17] Accordingly, during the hours of fasting, Muslims refrain not only from food and drink, but also from all behavior deemed to be [sinful in Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_views_on_sin).[18][19]
